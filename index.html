Case competition concept

A national impact map that makes Compudopt's local work feel visible, human, and scalable.

This proposal imagines a donor-friendly web experience where visitors hover over a state, see the cities and schools Compudopt has supported, and instantly understand the story behind the numbers. All school and impact data in this demo is fictional and included only for presentation purposes.

Why this concept works

  • Turns a national footprint into something interactive for judges, donors, and partners.
  • Combines emotional storytelling with credibility through school-level and city-level metrics.
  • Supports both hover exploration on desktop and click interaction on mobile devices.
  • Can later connect to real data from program operations, annual reports, or partner districts.
Suggested pitch: "Compudopt is already changing outcomes across communities. This map helps people see that impact, state by state."

Impact framing

What judges should be able to learn in seconds

Where Compudopt works

Visitors can scan the country, identify active states, and zoom into city-level relationships without reading a long report first.

Who is being reached

Each state can show students supported, households connected, devices distributed, and training sessions delivered.

Why the work matters

School cards can summarize fictional outcomes like attendance lift, homework completion, device access, and digital literacy gains.

Data model

Simple enough for a demo, expandable enough for the real thing

This prototype is powered by a single JavaScript object. Later, the same structure could be replaced by a CMS, Airtable, Google Sheets, or a nonprofit operations database.

State level

Name, short narrative, headline metrics, and whether the state has active data.

City level

Major service regions, hubs, or school district clusters that help visitors understand geographic spread.

School level

School names, device counts, student counts, and sample outcome language tailored for storytelling.
